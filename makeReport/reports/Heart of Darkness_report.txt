Heart of Darkness
=================

PROTAGONIST
-----------
Name: Charles Marlow
Description:
  A philosophical, observant, and experienced seaman who is deeply skeptical of
  imperialistic hypocrisy.
Primary goal:
  To navigate an African river to retrieve the enigmatic agent Kurtz and uncover the truth
  behind the colonial enterprise.

SUMMARY
-------
  Marlow takes a job as a riverboat captain for a European ivory trading company in
  Africa, where he witnesses the brutal and absurd realities of imperialism. He becomes
  fascinated by Kurtz, a legendary agent who has abandoned civilized morals to rule over a
  local tribe as a brutal demigod. After Kurtz's death, a disillusioned Marlow returns to
  Europe and lies to Kurtz's fiancée to protect her from the horrifying truth of human
  nature.

MAJOR THEMES
------------
- imperialism
- hypocrisy
- human darkness
- alienation
- truth versus illusion

MAJOR LOCATIONS
---------------
- The Thames River estuary
    Type: natural and public
    Significance: Serves as the framing setting, symbolizing the threshold between the
    civilized world and the dark unknown.
- The Company Offices
    Type: urban and institutional
    Significance: Represents the cold, bureaucratic, and hypocritical heart of the
    imperial machine.
- The African wilderness and river
    Type: natural and remote
    Significance: Symbolizes the untamed subconscious and a space devoid of societal
    restraints where true human nature is revealed.

CENTRAL CONFLICT
----------------
Summary:
  Marlow must navigate the physical dangers of the wilderness and the moral decay of the
  imperial agents while struggling to maintain his own sanity and ethical boundaries.
Structural type: person vs. self
Thematic opposing force:
  civilized illusion vs. primal savagery

PRINCIPAL EVENTS
----------------
- Marlow arrives at the outer stations and witnesses the brutal exploitation and death of
  the native workers.
    Relationship to values/goals: Shatters his belief in the civilizing mission and
    threatens his sense of justice and humanity.
- Marlow spends months repairing a sunken steamboat while observing the scheming and
  incompetence of the company agents.
    Relationship to values/goals: Highlights his reliance on efficiency and hard work as a
    means to maintain his sanity.
- The steamboat is attacked by natives en route to the Inner Station, resulting in the
  death of the helmsman.
    Relationship to values/goals: Tests his survival skills and deepens his understanding
    of the wilderness's overwhelming power.
- Marlow finally meets Kurtz and discovers the agent has descended into madness and brutal
  tyranny.
    Relationship to values/goals: Confronts Marlow with the ultimate corruption of the
    human soul when stripped of societal restraints.
- Following Kurtz's death, Marlow returns to Europe and lies to Kurtz's Intended about his
  final words.
    Relationship to values/goals: Forces Marlow to sacrifice his deep value of honesty to
    protect the fragile illusions of civilization.

VALUES PURSUED
--------------
- truth: Marlow seeks the reality behind the Company's noble rhetoric and Kurtz's legendary status.
- efficiency: Marlow uses practical work and competence as an anchor to maintain his sanity amidst chaos.

VALUES THREATENED
-----------------
- sanity: The absurd and brutal environment of the colonial stations threatens Marlow's mental stability.
- honesty: Marlow's commitment to the truth is ultimately sacrificed when he lies to Kurtz's Intended.

CONSEQUENCE AND RESOLUTION
--------------------------
Ending summary:
  Marlow returns to Europe profoundly alienated and lies to Kurtz's grieving fiancée,
  telling her that Kurtz's last word was her name rather than a declaration of horror.
Moral significance:
  Demonstrates that the dark truths of human nature are too devastating for civilized
  society, which relies on comforting illusions to survive.
