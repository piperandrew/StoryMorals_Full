IN_E_LifeClock
==============

PROTAGONIST
-----------
Name: Samar
Description:
  A young, anxious university student in Srinagar who is deeply infatuated with his
  classmate Rabiya.
Primary goal:
  To win Rabiya's love and build a relationship with her despite the surrounding social
  and political turmoil.

SUMMARY
-------
  In the politically volatile and militarized city of Srinagar, young student Samar falls
  in love with Rabiya, while a local metalworker named Mubarak struggles to keep his
  family afloat amidst curfews and personal temptations. As political unrest, violence,
  and communication blackouts paralyze the region, the characters' personal lives,
  livelihoods, and relationships are severely tested. Ultimately, the oppressive
  environment and societal pressures force Samar and Rabiya apart, leaving the characters
  to navigate a fractured, war-torn reality.

MAJOR THEMES
------------
- Political conflict and militarization
- The fragility of love
- Environmental and urban decay
- Social conservatism
- Survival amidst chaos

PRINCIPAL EVENTS
----------------
- Samar and Rabiya meet and slowly fall in love on a local matador, navigating the tense
  urban landscape of Srinagar. Establishes Samar's pursuit of love and connection amidst a
  chaotic environment.
- Mubarak's cow dies because strict curfews prevent him from obtaining necessary medicine.
  Demonstrates how political oppression directly threatens the survival and economic
  stability of ordinary families.
- Samar and Rabiya are publicly attacked and insulted for their romance. Highlights the
  threat of social conservatism and lack of personal freedom in their community.
- Sana discovers the militarized reality of Pari Mahal and uncovers Samar and Rabiya's
  secret romance. Marks the intersection of lost childhood innocence with the fragile,
  hidden nature of love in a conflict zone.
- Samar and Rabiya's relationship collapses due to family opposition and communication
  blackouts, forcing Samar to leave for Delhi. Culminates the destruction of personal
  dreams and autonomy by systemic conflict and societal pressure.

VALUES PURSUED
--------------
- Love: Samar relentlessly pursues a romantic connection with Rabiya as a source of hope and meaning.
- Normalcy: The characters strive to maintain everyday routines, businesses, and relationships despite the surrounding violence.
- Freedom: Characters seek the autonomy to choose their partners, move freely, and live without constant surveillance or restriction.

VALUES THREATENED
-----------------
- Safety: The constant presence of security bunkers, protests, curfews, and gunfire puts the characters' physical well-being at risk.
- Autonomy: Communication blackouts, curfews, and forced marriages strip the characters of their ability to make personal choices.
- Innocence: Children like Sana are forced to grow up in militarized zones, realizing early on that their world is dangerous and restricted.

CONSEQUENCE AND RESOLUTION
--------------------------
Ending summary:
  Samar flees to Delhi after his relationship with Rabiya is destroyed by family
  opposition and communication blackouts, while Rabiya is forced into an arranged marriage
  and violence continues to erupt in Srinagar.
